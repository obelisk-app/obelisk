## The relay is the server

In Discord, the "server" you join is Discord's server. In Obelisk, the "server" is **a Nostr relay**. Specifically, a relay that speaks **NIP-29** (relay-managed groups).

When you open the app you'll see a left-side rail with one or more icons. Each icon is a relay. Click one and you see the NIP-29 groups that exist on that relay. Click `+` and you add another. Click and hold to remove one. The app itself is the same code — what changes is *whose relay* is on the other end of the websocket.

## Why this matters

- **No vendor lock-in.** Move your community to a different relay if you don't like the policies of the current one. Your key, your group memberships, your follows — all portable.
- **No platform deplatforming.** We don't run any relay. There is no "Obelisk team" who can ban you off the network.
- **No platform deciding who can talk.** The relay's operator sets the rules for that relay. If you don't like them, run your own (`strfry`, `nostr-rs-relay`, and others all support NIP-29).

## Picking a relay

The default in the app is `wss://groups.fiatjaf.com` — a public NIP-29 relay run by the protocol's author. It works out of the box and is fine for trying things out.

For a real community you probably want one of:

1. **Your own relay.** A 5 USD/month VPS runs a NIP-29 relay easily for hundreds of users. You hold the data. You set the policy.
2. **A friendly public relay.** Many community-run NIP-29 relays exist; pick one whose operator you trust.
3. **A private relay.** If you only want a small invite-only group, run a relay that only accepts events from a fixed set of pubkeys.

## How permissions work

Inside a NIP-29 group, the relay enforces three roles:

- **Admins** — listed in a `39001` event. Can add members, kick, and ban.
- **Members** — listed in a `39002` event. Can post.
- **Everyone else** — depending on the group's metadata, may or may not read.

Kicking and banning are also Nostr events (`9001`, `9005`). The relay refuses to publish messages from people who aren't allowed to post.

This means:

- **Moderation is portable.** If you fork a community to a new relay, you replay the admin and member events to set up the same permissions there.
- **Moderation is auditable.** Every kick is a signed event with a timestamp and a reason.
- **Moderation is the relay's job, not the app's.** Obelisk doesn't enforce policies — the relay does.

## What about spam?

Right now, anti-spam is whatever your relay enforces. Most NIP-29 relays use proof-of-work, allow-lists, or invite-based join flows. We're tracking community work on Web of Trust scoring at the relay level — once relays expose a standard, the client can show trust scores in-line. (Earlier versions of Obelisk computed WoT in the backend; with no backend, the client now defers to whatever the relay enforces.)
