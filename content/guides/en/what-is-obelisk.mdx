## The short version

Obelisk is a chat client that looks and feels like Discord — servers in a left rail, channels, threads, reactions, DMs — but everything underneath is **Nostr**. There is no Obelisk backend, no database, no account system. Your identity is a Nostr keypair. Your groups are NIP-29 groups stored on Nostr relays. Every message you send is a signed Nostr event published straight to the relay.

## What that actually means

- **No signup.** You log in with a Nostr key (NIP-07 extension or nsec). There is no email, no password, no Obelisk account to create or recover.
- **No central server.** We don't run anything. You point the app at any NIP-29 relay you like — `wss://groups.fiatjaf.com` is the default, but you can add or replace it.
- **No database.** Channels, members, admins, kicks and bans aren't rows in our database — they're standard NIP-29 events the relay enforces.
- **Your data is portable.** Switch relays, run your own, mirror your group across many. The app doesn't own anything.

## Who is this for?

Crypto communities, privacy nerds, open-source teams, hackathons, and anyone allergic to "verify your email / confirm your phone" forms. If you already use Nostr, Obelisk is a familiar Discord-style face for groups you may already be part of. If you don't, the login screen will make you a key in two clicks.

<Callout type="info" title="No backend means">
We can't ban you. We can't read your DMs. We can't lose your messages — because we never had them. The relay you choose is what holds the data, and you can always pick a different relay.
</Callout>

## What's working today

- **Login** with NIP-07 or nsec
- **NIP-29 groups** — create, join, channel hierarchy, replies, reactions, admins, members
- **Multi-relay UX** — each relay you add appears as its own server in the rail
- **Encrypted DMs** via NIP-04 and NIP-17 (gift-wrapped)
- **Lightning zaps** from any message via NIP-57 + Nostr Wallet Connect (NIP-47)
- **Profile editor** that publishes a kind 0 event

## What's coming
