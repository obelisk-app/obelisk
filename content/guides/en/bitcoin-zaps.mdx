## What is a zap, really?

A **zap** is a Lightning payment that's also a public Nostr event. You pay sats to someone over Lightning, and the receipt — a signed `kind 9735` event — is published to the relay so everyone in the conversation sees that the sats moved.

In Obelisk, a zap is just a button on a message. Click it, pick an amount, your wallet signs and pays the invoice, the recipient's wallet receives, and the chat shows the zap inline.

## What's involved

Zapping uses two NIPs:

- **NIP-57** — defines the zap request/receipt event format and the LNURL flow that turns a Nostr identity into a Lightning invoice
- **NIP-47** — Nostr Wallet Connect (NWC), the protocol that lets the app ask your wallet to pay an invoice over Nostr, without ever holding your keys

Combined, they let Obelisk send sats from your wallet to anyone with a `lud16` (Lightning address) in their Nostr profile — without us touching the funds, the keys, or even the wallet.

## How it works in the app

1. **Connect a wallet.** Open settings, paste a Nostr Wallet Connect URI from any NWC-compatible wallet (Alby Hub, Mutiny, Phoenix, etc.). The URI is stored in your browser, encrypted to your Nostr key.
2. **Pick a message.** Click the zap button on a chat message.
3. **Pick an amount.** Default amounts are configurable; you can also enter a custom number.
4. **Sign and pay.** Obelisk builds a `kind 9734` zap request, sends it to the recipient's LNURL endpoint to get a Lightning invoice, and asks your wallet (over NWC) to pay the invoice.
5. **The receipt appears.** The recipient's LNURL endpoint publishes a `kind 9735` zap receipt to the relay. The chat shows the zap.

## What's private and what isn't

- **The amount and recipient are public.** Anyone reading the channel can see "Alice zapped Bob 1000 sats."
- **Your wallet's balance is private.** Obelisk never sees it — NWC just passes pay-invoice commands.
- **Your wallet keys are private.** They never leave your wallet. Obelisk only has permission to ask it to pay invoices, within whatever spend limit you set.
- **The Lightning routing is private** the same way any Lightning payment is — onion-routed, no on-chain footprint.

## What does it cost?

- **No platform fee.** Obelisk takes nothing. There is no platform.
- **Lightning routing fees.** A few sats at most for typical amounts.
- **Wallet fees.** Whatever your wallet provider charges, if anything.

## Why this matters

Zapping turns "real money" into a primitive of group chat. Tip a great post. Pay a bounty. Crowdfund a project. Run a paid Q&A. None of it requires a payment processor, a credit card, or a middleman who can freeze the transaction.
